Ratan Naval Tata

1937 – 2024

“I don’t believe in taking the right decisions. I take decisions and then make them right.”

Biography

Ratan Naval Tata (1937–2024) was an influential Indian industrialist and philanthropist who served as the chairman of the Tata Group from 1991 to 2012 and again from 2016 to 2017. During his tenure, the Tata Group expanded globally with acquisitions such as Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley, and Corus Steel.

Born in Mumbai, he studied architecture at Cornell University and completed an advanced management program at Harvard Business School. Under his leadership, Tata Motors introduced the affordable Tata Nano, showcasing his vision to make technology accessible to all.

Ratan Tata was a compassionate leader known for his humility, simplicity, and philanthropy. Through the Tata Trusts, he devoted much of his wealth to improving healthcare, education, and rural development. He received India’s highest civilian honors — the Padma Bhushan (2000) and the Padma Vibhushan (2008).

“Ups and downs in life are very important to keep us going, because a straight line even in an ECG means we are not alive.”

Life and Legacy

  • Born: December 28, 1937, Mumbai, India
  • Education: B.Arch, Cornell University; Management Program, Harvard Business School
  • Chairmanship: Tata Sons (1991–2012, 2016–2017)
  • Major Acquisitions: Jaguar Land Rover, Tetley, Corus Steel
  • Philanthropy: Healthcare, education, and animal welfare through Tata Trusts
  • Awards: Padma Bhushan (2000), Padma Vibhushan (2008)
